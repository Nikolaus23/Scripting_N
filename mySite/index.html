What is Biggar?

Biggar is a modest town in Saskatchewan, about an hour away from Saskatoon, with an odd name but much heart. You can tell as soon as you enter it and see their town slogan, “New York is big, but this is Biggar” when you see that, you should understand what this town is like.

Life in Biggar

Biggar, despite the name, is a relatively small town, and thus, like any other community of its size, the main street is Biggar's lifeblood, bringing everyone closer together. Walking through the main street can sometimes feel like travelling into the past as you see the buildings that have been there for as long as the street itself, reminding you about the history of this place.

At the south end of the main street, you can see where the Biggar train station once stood. Built in 1909, the rest of Biggar has grown from this train station. The town of Biggar started with a population of about 600 and reached 2 133 residences as of the 2021 census. The town of Biggar might not be big in size, but this town will always be Biggar in heart than most cities.
