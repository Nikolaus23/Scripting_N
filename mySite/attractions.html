Attractions

History

Biggar, despite its size, has a lot to do for anyone who lives there or is visiting. There is so much to do you would find it hard to be bored. Firstly, there is the main street, the town's lifeblood with historic buildings, shops and restaurants, everything you need.

Museum, Curling, and More

Just off the main street, you can see the Biggar Museum and Gallery, where you can see the recreation of the town's train station, which started the town of Biggar. The museum features a reconstruction of the Majestic Treater as it was in 1929 during the times of silent films, with the original piano giving visitors a chance to experience what theatregoing would have been like at that time.

Next, if you like sports such as curling, you must check out the Biggar Curling Rink, where you will see the town's deep love for the sport. Also, next to the curling rink, you can see the Jubilee Stadium, which in winter hosts ice hockey and in summer can hosts social events. Lastly, if you are the type of person who loves to go to parks, Biggar has plenty of them to go around. One park, in particular, is the Sandra Schmirler Olympic Gold Park, named after the town's Olympic curling gold medal winner in 1998. As you can see, there are plenty of things to see and do in Biggar to make the trip memorable.
