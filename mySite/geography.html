Geography

prairies

The geography of Biggar does not have the most enormous mountains or the deepest valleys, but what it lacks in scale, it makes up for in its simplicity. Being in the prairies, farm fields are cross-stitched across the flat lands as barley grows tall toward the sky.

Weather and Views

Despite being relatively flat, there still are lots of hills around Biggar, looking like waves frozen in time with wild grass and flowers growing on them. Additionally, scatterings of trees can be seen acting as islands of forest in the great and wide prairies of Saskatchewan. It often rains around the town, leaving the air heavy with the scent of petrichor whether indoors or out, making you feel more connected to the world around you. The area around Biggar is beautiful there is nothing quite like it, and it is worth experiencing just by itself.
